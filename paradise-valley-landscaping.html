Local Knowledge

What Paradise Valley Projects Require

Paradise Valley is Arizona's most distinctive landscape market. Lots are larger, homes are higher-value, and the expectations for finish quality, material consistency, and design cohesion are calibrated accordingly. Homeowners here aren't comparing us to the cheapest bidder — they're evaluating whether the team can execute at the level the property demands.

Most Paradise Valley properties we work on involve layered outdoor entertaining zones — distinct spaces for cooking, dining, lounging, and pool-adjacent living that connect naturally rather than feeling like separate rooms dropped into a yard. That kind of flow requires design intention from the start, not afterthought additions.

Material quality matters more here than anywhere else we work. Large-format travertine, custom stone coping, integrated fire elements, outdoor kitchen cabinetry that matches interior selections — these details are expected, not upgrades. We spec them correctly from the consultation forward so there are no surprises when the budget is finalized.

Privacy is also a consistent priority. Paradise Valley lots often back to other high-value properties or feature sightlines from neighboring homes. Effective privacy planting, pergola positioning, and fence or wall design are part of the design conversation from day one.

Investment Range

What Paradise Valley Projects Typically Cost

Full estate landscape builds in Paradise Valley typically start at $60,000 and commonly reach $150,000–$250,000+ for properties where the entire outdoor space is being designed and built from the ground up. Targeted outdoor kitchen and fire feature additions run $25,000–$60,000 depending on scope. Patio and hardscape upgrades run $15,000–$50,000 depending on square footage and material selection.

We provide a specific planning range during the consultation after evaluating the site. We don't quote off photos or square footage alone — Paradise Valley lots have too much variation in topography, soil, and utility placement for that approach to be useful.

Questions Paradise Valley Homeowners Ask

Targeted additions — outdoor kitchen in an existing space, lighting upgrade, pool surround remodel — are also common for homeowners who want to upgrade specific areas without a full-yard build.

Permitting requirements in Paradise Valley depend on the scope. Structural elements — pergolas, shade structures, retaining walls over certain heights — typically require Town of Paradise Valley permits. Gas line additions require mechanical permits. We handle permit applications as part of the project scope and build permit timelines into the construction schedule so there are no delays after you've committed.

Privacy planning starts at the design phase — not after the hardscape is done. We evaluate sightlines from neighboring properties, determine effective planting heights and growth rates for privacy screens, and position pergolas and shade structures to block problem views without enclosing the space. The goal is privacy that feels natural rather than a fence disguised as planting.
